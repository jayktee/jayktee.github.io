I write about data science, politics and maps.

I’m a researcher at the Lane Center at Stanford where I research policing reform in America. Before coming to Stanford, I spent 10 years in education and development policy in Southeast Asia at the Malaysian Prime Minister’s Department, McKinsey & Company and Teach For Malaysia.

At Stanford, I also teach classes at the Public Policy department in data analysis and statistical methods. I am an alum of the Stanford Data Lab and I am the data and dev lead for an ongoing volunteer project called Bay Area Community to collate critical resources for the Bay Area.

I hold double Masters degrees in Education (Policy, Organization and Leadership Studies) and Public Policy from Stanford, and have an undergraduate degree in Political Science at Yale.
